Which UML models are most applicable at different stages of the software development life cycle?

Based on our current reading up to this point, we were asked to consider the different types of UML diagrams and at which point they were most applicable within the software development life cycle (SDLC).

Use Case Diagrams

The use case diagram is most applicable during requirements analysis. It is used to establish who the users are and how they will interact with the system. This provides a high-level view which maps the expected users to parts of a system, prompting consideration of how they will make use of it and the interfaces which need to exist to facilitate that usage.

Activity Diagrams

The activity diagram is applicable during the earlier stages of design. It is used to show the relationship between different activities taking place within a system during execution. The activity diagram shows a more detailed level of interaction than the use case diagram by showing specific stimuli and decision points with possible reactions in a way which resembles a flow chart.

Class Diagrams

The class diagram is used later, towards the end of design and near or during implementation. It describes the objects which will be included in the system in detail, including the expected properties and methods of each object. It also details how objects will interact and maps traits such as inheritance, dependence and inclusion. The class diagram aids development by ensuring developers have visibility of the required functionality of a system, or at least the relevant part of a system, during development.
