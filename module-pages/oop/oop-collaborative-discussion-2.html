Collaborative Discussion 2

Our second collaborative discussion, started in Unit 8, involved reviewing the article: 'Towards a Development Methodology for Smart Object-Oriented IoT Systems: a Metamodel Approach' and considering the strengths and weaknesses of designing a metamodel to support obect-oriented design of the Internet of Things. In addition to this, we were asked to design our own smart model, as seen in Figure 6 of the article.

Initial Posting

Initial Post
by Elliot Bedworth - Monday, 2 October 2023, 10:02 PM

The article proposes an interesting approach to designing Smart Objects (SOs), which themselves are an important object oriented design element for IoT development.

This was my first exposure to the metamodel approach and I feel that the article does well in showing its strengths. Through modelling the SOs at different levels of abstraction, through the varying stages of development, a broad standardisation is developed which allows for high levels of consistency across development teams and applications. This in itself also contributes to building a clear design roadmap, which will enable clearer setting of project milestones against the requirements for each model. In addition to this, the approach of modelling the models at increasing levels of granularity introduces a higher-level view of each stage of development. I believe this will benefit teams by both prompting more forethought in the early stages of design, and by enabling more efficient changes at a later development stage (if/when required) by providing such a view.

Considering the approach further, I do have reservations about the level of inflexibility in such an approach. If the metamodel approach is to be applied as intended, across many instantiations of the object model being modelled, where do developers turn if their requirements do not align with the existing metamodel? I would expect this to require deviation from the metamodel, or creation of an entirely new SO which may share much functionality with the slightly different existing model. This of course would create additional (re)work, as opposed to improving the design process.
